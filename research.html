Research Experience

Academic research in medical robotics and device development

Flexible AI-Enabled Mechatronics Systems Laboratory

Research Assistant | December 2025 - Present

In the Flexible AI Lab, I contribute to the development of next-generation robotic endoscopes for minimally invasive surgeries. My work focuses on overcoming the mechanical challenges of scaling down medical devices while maintaining functionality and biocompatibility.

Key Contributions

Biocompatible Tube Development

Designed flexible tubes scalable to 1.3mm diameter, targeting FDA clearance requirements for urological applications. This involved material selection, finite element analysis of flexibility requirements, and prototyping validation.

Hydraulic Actuation System

Collaborated with robotics engineers to miniaturize hydraulic actuation mechanisms, enabling precise control necessary for delicate surgical procedures. Our system balances force transmission with size constraints unique to medical applications.
