Soft Robotic Humanoid Hand

Mechanical Lead | Sept - Dec 2025 | Team of 30

As Mechanical Engineering Team Lead, I directed the design and fabrication of a fully functional humanoid hand for prosthetic and rehabilitative applications. This project represented a significant step toward affordable, accessible assistive technology.

Design Approach

The hand assembly was designed in SolidWorks with dual-material construction: PETG-CF filament for structural durability and TPU 95-A for flexible skin overlays. This combination provided the necessary toughness for motor integration while maintaining comfortable human-robot interaction.

Manufacturing Innovation

We produced ten silicone-molded fingers using custom 3D-printed molds, with separate assemblies for pneumatic actuation and sensor integration. Each finger required four precision mold parts, demanding tight tolerances and careful material selection for optimal biocompatibility.

Technical Impact

The completed system successfully integrated pneumatic tubing, haptic feedback motors, and flexible sensors through collaboration with electrical engineering students. The hand demonstrated promising applications across prosthetics, humanoid robotics, and rehabilitative devices.
